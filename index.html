About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Private Markets Department (DEC–IFC) of the World Bank Group, based in Paris. My research combines empirical and theoretical methods to study production networks, trade patterns, and spatial development — with a particular interest in how shocks propagate through the global economy.

I hold a PhD in Economics (cum laude) from the Sant'Anna School of Advanced Studies in Pisa, where I graduated in July 2025 under the supervision of Giorgio Fagiolo. I also hold an MSc in Statistics from ETH Zürich and a BSc in Mathematics and Economics from the University of Leeds. Before my current position, I was a PhD Trainee at the European Central Bank, working on non-bank financial institutions (NBFIs) and financial derivatives, and a Short-Term Consultant at the World Bank, working on resilient infrastructure and supply chains.
