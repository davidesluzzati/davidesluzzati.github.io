About

I am an economist and Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Development Economics Vice Presidency at the World Bank Group, based in Paris. My research combines empirical and theoretical methods to study production networks, trade patterns, and spatial development — with a particular interest in how shocks propagate through the global economy.

I hold a PhD in Economics (cum laude) from the Sant'Anna School of Advanced Studies in Pisa, with a dissertation on economic networks supervised by Giorgio Fagiolo. I also hold an MSc in Statistics from ETH Zürich and a BSc in Mathematics and Economics from the University of Leeds. Before the World Bank, I worked at the European Central Bank on the EMIR derivatives database and held research positions spanning complexity economics, climate resilience of infrastructure, and network analysis.
